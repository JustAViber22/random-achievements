## What Random Achievements brings to Minecraft

**Random Achievements** adds **silly, funny, and sometimes helpful achievements** to Minecraft. When you complete one, you get a **reward** (items). Progress is shown in chat: press **J** or run **/ra progress** to see the full list. Everything appears in chat.

### Why you might want Random Achievements

I originally made this mod because everytime I would play with my friends, we'd find that Survival lacks side quests and goals. So if you want extra goals in survival with your friends (that are sometimes **a bit** ridiculous), or you want a light layer of "achievements with rewards" without changing core gameplay, this mod adds that. It works in singleplayer and on server; and the progress is saved per player.

### What you need to know before downloading

- **Requires Fabric** for Minecraft 1.21.x and **Fabric API**.
- **Client and server:** Install on both if you play multiplayer; the progress is stored on the server.
- **Progress resets on death!** (by design) Random Achievements are meant to be re-earnable.
- **Hidden Achievements** Certain achievements do **NOT** have a description (replaced by ???). You'll have to mess around to figure out how to get them!

# How to use

- **View your Progress:** Press **J** (default key) or run `/ra progress`. The list of random achievements appears in chat; scroll up if the list cuts off.
- **In-game help:** Run `/ra` in the chat to see a short reminder.
- Completing an achievement automatically grants the reward and shows a toast; nothing is needed to claim!

# Technical notes

### Installation
1. Have [Fabric Loader](https://fabricmc.net/use/installer/) installed for Minecraft 1.21.10.
2. Download the Random Achievements Mod (.jar File)
3. Place the .jar File in your (**Windows:**`%appdata%\.minecraft\mods` **other:** `.minecraft\mods`) folder. 
4. Open Minecraft and Enjoy!

### Other Information
- Keybind is in the **Random Achievements** category in Controls. You can rebind the progress key from **J** to anything you like.
- Data is stored per player via Fabric’s attachment system and persists with the world/server.
